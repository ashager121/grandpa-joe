Grandpa Joe

In both versions of the tale, it is very clear who is number one in Grandpa Joe's mind. In both versions he decides he must accompany Charlie to the chocolate factory. In the movie, he actually speaks of the ticket as though he, not Charlie, was the winner. He considers eating everything a viable option. He doesn't pause to consider that others might be hungry. He puts his immediate desire for fizzy lifting drinks ahead of Charlie's lifetime supply of chocolate. In short, he is worried about himself first and everyone else is a distant second. Even when Wonka give Charlie the factory his first thought is about himself and he never even asks about the rest of the family, not even his own wife.
